Why LegacyBridge exists

People hesitate to put financial data on third-party servers.

When financial and wealth information is stored with a third-party company, users worry about trust, misuse, spam emails, unsolicited marketing calls and data exposure.

LegacyBridge is built around that pain. Your asset information is not kept as readable data on our server. It is encrypted and saved in the Google Drive of the user account with which you log in.

Data ownership

Not our Drive. Not a public folder. Your Google Drive AppData folder.

LegacyBridge uses Google sign-in so your encrypted vault can be saved in the private app storage area of the Google Drive account you connect.

01

User logs in with Google

The user connects the Google account where the encrypted LegacyBridge vault will be stored.

02

Data is encrypted first

Financial records are encrypted in the browser before they are saved outside the user’s device.

03

Saved to Drive AppData

The encrypted file is stored in the user’s Google Drive AppData folder, which is not visible like a normal Drive folder.

04

Readable only through app

The vault can be viewed only after login and decryption using the correct secret passphrase or verified claim process.
